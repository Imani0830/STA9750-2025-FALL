Streaming to the Top: How Netflix Originals Conquered

Summary

As part of Netflix’s ongoing investment in high quality, globally appealing content this report provides a data driven analysis of the most successful shows and films from our Top 10 rankings.

Using publicly available Netflix Top 10 data, it has been examined for standout titles that reached the #1 spot after debuting lower in the rankings, charted in the most countries during their first week, accumulated the highest global viewership hours,had record setting runs in individual country Top 10 lists.

These findings support upcoming Public Relations campaigns by quantifying recent content successes, reinforcing Netflix’s commitment to accessible, mass market entertainment with global impact.

Analyzing the Data: Key Questions and Insights

Question 1

How many different countries does Netflix operate in?

Show the code 
COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  distinct(country_name)

Netflix operates in 94 countries.

Question 2

Which non-English-language film has spent the most cumulative weeks in the global top 10? How many weeks did it spend?

Show the code 
GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  select(show_title, weekly_rank, cumulative_weeks_in_top_10, category) |>
  filter(category == "Films (Non-English)") |>
  slice_max(cumulative_weeks_in_top_10)

The non-English-language film that spent the most cumulative weeks in the global Top 10 is All Quiet on the Western Front, with a total of 23 weeks.

Question 3

What is the longest film (English or non-English) to have ever appeared in the Netflix global Top 10? How long is it in minutes?

Show the code 
GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  select(show_title, weekly_rank, runtime) |>
  slice_max(runtime)

Until You Burn is the longest film to have appeared in the global Top 10. Its runtime is 47.6 minutes.

Question 4

For each of the four categories, what program has the most total hours of global viewership?

Show the code 
GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  distinct(category, weekly_hours_viewed) |>
  select(category, weekly_hours_viewed) |>
  slice_max(weekly_hours_viewed)

The program category TV (Non-English) has the most total hours of global viewership.

Question 5

Which TV show had the longest run in a country’s Top 10? How long was this run and in what country did it occur?

Show the code 
COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  select(show_title, category, cumulative_weeks_in_top_10, country_name) |>
  filter(category == "TV") |>
  slice_max(cumulative_weeks_in_top_10)
Show the code 
COUNTRY_TOP_10|>
  distinct(category)

The TV show Money Heist had the longest run in a country’s Top 10. It spent 127 weeks in the Top 10 in Pakistan.

Question 6

Netflix provides over 200 weeks of service history for all but one country in our data set. Which country is this and when did Netflix cease operations in that country?

Show the code 
COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  group_by(country_name) |>
  summarise(weeks_of_data = n_distinct(week), latest_date = max(week, na.rm = TRUE), .groups = 'drop') |>
  arrange(weeks_of_data)

Netflix ceased operations in Russia. The last date it serviced the country was 2022-02-27, with only 35 weeks of data reported.

Question 7

What is the total viewership of the TV show Squid Game? Note that there are three seasons total and we are looking for the total number of hours watched across all seasons.

Show the code 
GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  filter(show_title == "Squid Game") |>
  group_by(show_title) |>
  summarise(sum(weekly_views, na.rm = TRUE))

The total viewership of the TV show Squid Game is 3.969^{8}.

Question 8

The movie Red Notice has a runtime of 1 hour and 58 minutes. Approximately how many views did it receive in 2021? Note that Netflix does not provide the weekly_views values that far back in the past, but you can compute it yourself using the total view time and the runtime.

Show the code 
red_notice_runtime_hours <- 118 / 60 

GLOBAL_TOP_10 |>
  filter(show_title == "Red Notice",
         year(week) == 2021) |>
  summarise(
    total_hours = sum(weekly_hours_viewed, na.rm = TRUE),
    estimated_views = total_hours / red_notice_runtime_hours
  )

The movie Red Notice had approximately 2.017322^{8} total views in 2021.

Question 9

How many Films reached Number 1 in the US but did not originally debut there? That is, find films that first appeared on the Top 10 chart at, e.g., Number 4 but then became more popular and eventually hit Number 1? What is the most recent film to pull this off?

Show the code 
us_films <- COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  filter(country_name == "United States", category == "Films")

us_films |>
  group_by(show_title) |>
  summarise(
    debut_week = min(week),
    debut_rank = weekly_rank[which.min(week)][1],
    hit_number_1 = any(weekly_rank == 1),
    first_week_at_1 = if (any(weekly_rank == 1)) min(week[weekly_rank == 1]) else as.Date(NA),
    .groups = "drop"
  ) |>
  filter(debut_rank > 1, hit_number_1 == TRUE) |>
  arrange(desc(first_week_at_1)) |>
  slice(1)

45 films reached number 1 in the U.S. but did not originally debut there. Unknown Number: The High is the most recent film to have pulled this off. It debuted at number 4 during the week of 2025-08-31, and first reached number 1 in the week of 2025-09-07.

Question 10

Which TV show/season hit the top 10 in the most countries in its debut week? In how many countries did it chart?

Show the code 
library(dplyr)

COUNTRY_TOP_10 |>
  filter(category == "TV") |>
  
  group_by(show_title, season_title) |>
  mutate(debut_week = min(week)) |>
  ungroup() |>
    filter(week == debut_week) |>
    group_by(show_title, season_title, debut_week) |>
    summarise(
      countries_in_debut_week = n_distinct(country_name),
      .groups = "drop"
    ) |>
    arrange(desc(countries_in_debut_week)) |>
    slice(1)

In its debut week of 2021-12-26, Emily in Paris, Season 2 hit the Top 10 in the most countries — 94 countries.

Press Release 2: Commercial Success in India

Streaming the Subcontinent: Netflix Surges Ahead in India’s Entertainment Market

Sunday, October 5, 2025

NETFLIX

Popular Films - India

Netflix’s continued investment in Hindi-language content is paying off with a growing library of homegrown films finding enormous success in India’s Top 10 rankings, even without global exposure.

  • Recent data reveals that over 20 films charted consistently in India but, never appeared in the U.S. Top 10, underscoring the unique tastes of Indian viewers and the platform’s deepening cultural resonance in the region.

  • Titles like “Haseen Dillruba”, The Great Indian Kapil Show, The Railway Men- Untold Story of Bhopal 1984 dominated India’s weekly Top 10 for 8 or more weeks, often reaching the #1 spot and sustaining high ranks throughout their run.

  • These films didn’t chart as much internationally, yet drew enough local attention to suggest strong user engagement, potentially representing millions of Indian subscribers actively watching regional content.

  • With India now the most populous country in the world, Netflix’s focus on high quality Hindi films positions it for significant subscriber growth. The data driven success of these titles confirms that localized storytelling is not just working, it’s winning.
